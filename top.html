Top 10 International Restaurants of 2025

By Tastadoo Team

Discover the crème de la crème of global dining—ten extraordinary restaurants that push the boundaries of culinary creativity, embodying the essence of their respective regions. These restaurants are not just about food; they are about creating memories that linger long after the meal is over. Here’s a curated list of the Top 10 International Restaurants of 2025, each offering its own unique flavor of excellence.

1. Osteria Aurora — Florence, Italy

A sun-kissed hilltop gem overlooking olive groves and vineyards.

Osteria Aurora isn’t just a restaurant—it’s a time machine draped in terracotta and ivy. As you walk through the arched wooden doors, you’re welcomed with warm scents of rosemary, garlic, and fresh bread baked in a stone oven. Chef Lucia Ventresca reimagines old Tuscan family recipes with light, modern touches. Her wild mushroom ravioli with truffle foam melts on the tongue like a whisper of the forest. Most guests dine slowly, sipping Chianti from hand-blown glass, while the golden hour spills across the countryside. It’s romance and rustic elegance on a plate.

Osteria Aurora's dining room, with views of rolling vineyards.

2. Umami Cloud — Tokyo, Japan

Where cuisine becomes meditation.

Tucked away in a minimalist penthouse above Tokyo’s neon-lit streets, Umami Cloud is more than a restaurant—it’s an experience of stillness and wonder. The decor is pure Zen: tatami floors, soft paper lanterns, and ambient forest sounds. Chef Kenta Yamura serves only 12 guests per evening in a silent, meditative environment where each dish is crafted as a moment of stillness. The sea urchin served in its shell, topped with matcha-infused crystals, bursts with umami flavor that clears the mind and resets the senses. Guests leave feeling rejuvenated, as if they’ve just experienced the essence of Japanese tranquility. A hidden sanctuary for the soul.

The minimalist dining experience at Umami Cloud in Tokyo.

3. Le Jardin Secret — Paris, France

A secret garden that whispers the romance of Paris.

Hidden behind ivy-clad gates, Le Jardin Secret is a Parisian gem known only to those with a true love for food and beauty. The restaurant is tucked within a century-old townhouse, where a private garden blooms year-round. Chef Thierry Rousseau, who grew up in the Loire Valley, brings the fresh vibrancy of French countryside ingredients into every dish. His signature dish, an artful duet of lobster and asparagus in a delicate saffron sauce, delights not just the palate but the eyes. Diners enjoy their meals under the canopy of roses and jasmine, where Parisian city lights flicker in the distance. Each bite carries the weight of French culinary history, yet feels new with every season’s harvest.

The romantic outdoor seating at Le Jardin Secret, Paris.

4. Narisawa — Tokyo, Japan

A masterful celebration of nature's bounty.

Narisawa is a symphony of flavors that celebrates Japan’s natural world. Chef Yoshihiro Narisawa blends local ingredients and innovative techniques to create dishes that evoke the changing seasons of Japan. His signature “Bread of the Earth,” a warm, earthy loaf of bread served with an accompanying edible soil made from mushrooms and herbs, is a reminder of the intimate relationship between food and nature. Every course at Narisawa feels like a journey through Japan’s pristine forests, mountain ranges, and seas, making every bite feel profoundly connected to the land.

“Essence of the Forest” course at Narisawa

5. D.O.M. — São Paulo, Brazil

Brazilian culinary ingenuity at its finest.

Chef Alex Atala’s D.O.M. offers an unparalleled culinary experience rooted in the diverse ecosystems of Brazil. Focusing on native ingredients, many of which have never been explored outside the country, Atala elevates local produce into artful dishes that challenge our understanding of flavor. His famed “Brazilian Amazonian Fish” takes indigenous ingredients like banana leaf and palm hearts, combining them with fish caught from the river, creating a sensory celebration of Brazil’s wilderness. D.O.M. is an embodiment of Atala’s mission to elevate Brazilian cuisine to global prominence.

The fancy atmosphere at D.O.M. in São Paulo, Brazil, adds to its world-renowned dining experience.

6. El Celler de Can Roca — Girona, Spain

A family story told through unforgettable plates.

El Celler de Can Roca is the culmination of a family’s dedication to food. Brothers Joan, Josep, and Jordi Roca have created a restaurant where each dish tells a personal story—whether it's the “Menu del Mar,” which brings the flavors of the Mediterranean to life, or the decadent “Liquid Olive” that captures the essence of Spanish olive oil in an entirely new form. It’s an intimate experience that’s more than just eating; it’s about savoring the memories and inspirations of three generations of Roca family cooking.

The creative presentation at El Celler de Can Roca, Spain.

7. Eleven Madison Park — New York, USA

Where luxury meets simplicity.

Eleven Madison Park has long been regarded as one of the finest dining establishments in the world. Chef Daniel Humm’s commitment to ingredient-driven cooking brings out the beauty in simplicity. Whether it's his famous honey lavender glazed duck or his innovative take on classic American dishes, each dish at Eleven Madison Park is a balance of luxury and restraint. The restaurant’s chic, minimalist ambiance complements the experience, offering an elevated but approachable taste of New York’s culinary scene.

Elegant dining at Eleven Madison Park, New York.

8. The French Laundry — Yountville, USA

A Californian vineyard dining experience.

Set in the heart of California’s Napa Valley, The French Laundry is a restaurant where fine dining is taken to an extraordinary level. Chef Thomas Keller’s mastery of French techniques combined with California’s freshest local ingredients results in a gastronomic experience that’s both refined and innovative. Known for its exquisite tasting menus, each plate is a work of art that reflects the local terroir—whether it's the signature “Oysters and Pearls” or the perfectly seared foie gras. A dining experience at The French Laundry is a journey into the heart of culinary artistry.

The refined dining room of The French Laundry, Yountville.

9. Attica — Melbourne, Australia

An adventurous exploration of Australian flavors.

Attica is a bold celebration of Australia’s native ingredients, bringing to the forefront flavors that have been hidden in plain sight. Chef Ben Shewry takes diners on a culinary journey that mixes indigenous bush foods with modern techniques. Signature dishes such as the “Saltbush and Wattleseed Lamb” showcase the rich diversity of Australian cuisine, blending rustic local flavors with contemporary elegance. Attica’s commitment to innovation makes it one of the most exciting dining experiences in the Southern Hemisphere.

The adventurous spirit of Attica’s cuisine, Melbourne, Australia.

10. Mirazur — Menton, France

A French Riviera experience with the freshest Mediterranean flavors.

Overlooking the beautiful coast of Menton, Mirazur’s breathtaking location is matched only by its world-class cuisine. Chef Mauro Colagreco’s menu is inspired by the pristine landscapes that surround his restaurant, with dishes featuring the finest seafood and locally sourced herbs and vegetables. His “Caviar of Sea Urchin” is one of the most coveted dishes in the world, offering a delicate balance of oceanic flavor and luxury. Dining at Mirazur is like tasting the very essence of the Mediterranean itself.

The coastal beauty and stunning cuisine at Mirazur, Menton, France.
