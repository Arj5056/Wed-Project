Cheese and Cranberry

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry store-bought or homemade
  • 4 oz. brie cheese
  • 4 tablespoons cranberry sauce
  • 1 egg for egg wash
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary optional

Directions

Step 1

Begin by preheating your oven to 200°C/400°F (fan) to ensure it reaches the correct temperature for baking. While the oven heats up, prepare a baking tray by lining it with either baking parchment or a silicone liner , which will prevent the pastry from sticking and make cleanup much easier later.

Step 2

Unroll the sheet of pastry carefully onto a clean work surface to keep it from tearing. Lightly brush the entire surface with egg wash, ensuring even coverage for a golden finish. Then, using a sharp knife or pastry cutter, divide the pastry into approximately 40 small, even-sized rectangles.

Step 3

To create a layered effect, take one rectangle of pastry and place it directly on top of another, pressing them gently together . Repeat this process with the remaining pieces of pastry until you have about 20 ‘doubled up’ rectangles, which will puff up nicely during baking.

Step 4

Arrange the layered pastry rectangles on the baking tray you prepared earlier, spacing them out slightly to allow room for expansion. Bake them in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes or until they are golden brown and puffed up. Remove them from the oven but keep the tray handy.

Step 5

While the pastry puffs are baking, slice the Brie into small, uniform slices so they will melt evenly. Once the pastries are out of the oven, place a slice of Brie on top of each puff. Return the tray to the oven for 1–2 minutes, just long enough for the Brie to soften and melt slightly.

Step 6

Finish and Serve: Carefully transfer the warm pastry puffs to a serving plate. Add a small dollop of cranberry sauce to the top of each one, followed by a few fresh rosemary leaves for a garnish that adds flavor and visual appeal. Serve the puffs warm as a delicious appetizer or snack
